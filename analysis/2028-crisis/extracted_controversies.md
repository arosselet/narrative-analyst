# Extracted Controversies: The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis

This document isolates the core disputes identified in the source text, focusing purely on extraction prior to macro-level narrative analysis.

## 1. The Human Displacement Spiral (Labor/GDP Mismatch)
The foundational dispute over whether AI productivity gains benefit the broader economy or destroy the human consumer base.
- **Specific Claims / Context:** AI allows for "OpEx substitution" where companies cut human headcount to buy more AI, leading to record margins but collapsing real wage growth. The result is "Ghost GDP"—output that doesn't circulate in the real economy because machines don't spend on discretionary goods.

## 2. The Intermediation Collapse (The "Death of Friction")
The conflict between traditional rent-extracting business models and friction-eliminating AI consumer agents.
- **Specific Claims / Context:** Agents running 24/7 optimize for price and fee elimination, destroying moats built on "habitual loyalty" or "tedious complexity." This impacts travel booking, insurance renewals, real estate commissions, delivery apps (DoorDash/Uber Eats), and credit card interchange fees (Mastercard/Visa shifting to stablecoins).

## 3. The Systemic Credit Contagion (Private Credit & Insurers)
The dispute over the resilience of private credit models when underlying software revenues evaporate.
- **Specific Claims / Context:** PE-backed software loans underwritten on "Annual Recurring Revenue" (ARR) begin to default (e.g., Zendesk). The "Permanent Capital" meant to absorb these losses is revealed to be the life insurance annuities of American households, leading to a regulatory crisis over risk-based capital treatment.

## 4. The Mortgage Bedrock Crisis
The tension between 30-year static credit assumptions and structural, permanent human income impairment.
- **Specific Claims / Context:** Prime borrowers (780+ FICO scores in tech/finance hubs) experience a structural loss of income, forcing underwriters to question the $13 trillion residential mortgage market. Borrowers are forced to drain savings and halt discretionary spending, accelerating the real economy's decline.

## 5. The Policy & Fiscal Failure
The struggle to adapt a 20th-century tax and social safety system to a post-labor economy.
- **Specific Claims / Context:** The government's revenue base (income and payroll taxes) shrinks just as the need for safety net outlays explodes. This leads to polarized political brinkmanship between proposals for direct transfers (Transition Economy Act), radical wealth redistribution from AI returns (Shared AI Prosperity Act), and warnings of Marxist regulatory capture.
