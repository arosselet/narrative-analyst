This is not a "restart" that happens with the flip of a switch. It is a multi-month technical odyssey of recalibrating the most sensitive equipment on the planet. Even if QatarEnergy announces a full restart tomorrow, the "revalidation lag" has already been baked into the system.

For chipmakers, the economic incentive to buy "cheap" Qatari helium consistently overrode the strategic necessity of building more expensive, domestic recycling infrastructure or sourcing from more stable—but higher-cost—producers in North America. The current crisis isn't a "shock" from the outside; it is the inevitable bill coming due for a decade of prioritizing margin over muscle.
